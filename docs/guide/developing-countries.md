# Developing-systems segment

A separate tracked set answering: what do lower-performing-but-improving systems
lack versus stronger peers, and what could draw youth to study there?

## Membership

Two ways in (a country can be both):

- **Criteria flag:** EPI in the bottom 30% of the cohort **and** a positive
  5-year EPI delta - i.e. genuinely lower-performing yet improving.
- **Curated watchlist:** hand-maintained in
  [`data/config/watchlist.json`](../../data/config/watchlist.json) (e.g. Malta,
  Georgia), each with explicit benchmark pairings and a rationale.

## Dual benchmark

Each developing system is compared against:

- a **geographic neighbor** (an advanced nearby system), and
- a **size-matched aspirational peer** (a comparable small/agile system).

The view shows the EPI gap and per-pillar gaps, the features the benchmarks have
that the country lacks, and **cited youth-motivator notes** (cost advantage,
niche standout programs, scholarships, pathways).

## Honesty

- Benchmark choices are interpretive and documented in the watchlist; the
  comparison is **descriptive, not a verdict**.
- Small-n: no statistical claims, only descriptive gaps.
- Narratives and motivators are cited; missing items show "not available".

Example seed entries: **Malta** (EU, English-medium, fast international growth)
and **Georgia** (medical-education engine), both benchmarked against a neighbor
and a peer with sourced reasoning.
